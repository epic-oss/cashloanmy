Your credit score isn't permanent. Whether you have late payments, defaults, or just a thin credit file, you can improve it. This guide shows you exactly how — with realistic timelines and proven strategies.

<Callout type="info" title="Good News">
Credit scores reflect recent behavior more than ancient history. 12 months of perfect payments can dramatically change how lenders see you.
</Callout>

## Understanding Your Starting Point

Before improving, know where you stand:

### CCRIS vs CTOS

| | CCRIS | CTOS |
|---|-------|------|
| What it is | Payment history record | Credit score + legal records |
| Score? | No score, just records | Yes (300-850) |
| Check cost | Free (eCCRIS) | RM24.85-49.90 |
| What banks see | Payment codes (0,1,2,3...) | Score + full report |

### CTOS Score Ranges

| Score | Rating | Loan Approval |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 750-850 | Excellent | Easy approval, best rates |
| 700-749 | Good | Most loans approved |
| 650-699 | Fair | Approved with conditions |
| 600-649 | Poor | Limited options |
| 300-599 | Very Poor | Very difficult |

### CCRIS Payment Codes

| Code | Meaning | Impact |
|------|---------|--------|
| 0 | Paid on time | Good ✓ |
| 1 | 1-29 days late | Minor concern |
| 2 | 30-59 days late | Warning sign |
| 3+ | 60+ days late | Serious problem |

## Quick Wins (1-3 Months)

Start here for fastest impact:

### 1. Pay Everything On Time

This is 90% of credit improvement. Set up:
- Auto-debit for all loans
- Calendar reminders 5 days before due date
- SMS alerts from your bank

**Impact:** Immediate — every on-time payment replaces a bad month in your 12-month history.

### 2. Pay Credit Card Before Statement Date

Not just before due date — before statement generates.

**Why:** Banks report your balance on statement date. If you pay before statement, your "utilization" shows as lower.

**Example:**
- Credit limit: RM10,000
- You spent: RM8,000 (80% utilization — bad)
- Pay RM6,000 before statement date
- Statement shows: RM2,000 (20% utilization — good)

### 3. Reduce Credit Card Utilization

Keep balances below 30% of limit on ALL cards.

| Utilization | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| 0-30% | Excellent |
| 30-50% | Acceptable |
| 50-70% | Concerning |
| 70%+ | Damaging |

**Quick fix:** Request credit limit increase (lowers utilization instantly without paying anything).

### 4. Don't Close Old Accounts

Old accounts with good history help your score. Closing them:
- Reduces total credit available
- Increases utilization percentage
- Removes positive history

**Exception:** Close if annual fee isn't worth it.

### 5. Stop Applying for New Credit

Every application creates an inquiry. Multiple inquiries = looks desperate.

**Rule:** No new applications for 3-6 months while rebuilding.

<Callout type="tip" title="Quick Win Summary">
Pay on time + reduce credit card balances + stop applying = visible improvement in 1-3 months.
</Callout>

## Medium-Term Strategies (3-6 Months)

### 6. Dispute Errors on Your Report

Errors happen. Common ones:
- Account you never opened (possible fraud)
- Wrong payment status
- Closed account showing as open
- Duplicate entries

**How to dispute CCRIS:**
1. Identify the error
2. Contact the bank that reported it (not BNM)
3. Provide evidence (receipts, statements)
4. Bank investigates and updates if valid
5. Allow 2-4 weeks

**How to dispute CTOS:**
1. Log in to MyCTOS
2. Click "Dispute" next to incorrect item
3. Upload supporting documents
4. CTOS contacts data provider
5. Resolution in 14-30 days

### 7. Settle Outstanding Defaults

If you have defaults/write-offs:

1. Contact the lender
2. Negotiate settlement amount (often 50-70% of original)
3. Get settlement letter IN WRITING
4. Pay the agreed amount
5. Request they update CCRIS status

**Note:** Settled defaults still show on record, but "settled" looks better than "outstanding."

### 8. Become an Authorized User

Ask a family member with excellent credit to add you as authorized user on their credit card.

**Benefits:**
- Their good payment history may help yours
- Builds your credit file
- No risk to them if set up correctly

**Important:** Only works if the bank reports authorized users to credit bureaus (not all do).

### 9. Get a Secured Credit Card

If you can't get approved for regular cards:

1. Apply for secured credit card
2. Put down deposit (becomes your limit)
3. Use card for small purchases
4. Pay in full every month
5. Builds positive payment history

**Banks offering secured cards:** CIMB, Maybank, some others — ask specifically.

## Long-Term Strategies (6-12 Months)

### 10. Build Payment History Length

The longer your history of on-time payments, the better.

| History Length | Impact |
|----------------|--------|
| 6 months clean | Shows improvement |
| 12 months clean | Significant impact |
| 24 months clean | Excellent standing |

**Patience required:** There's no shortcut to time.

### 11. Diversify Credit Types

Having different credit types (managed well) helps:
- Credit card
- Personal loan
- Car loan
- Mortgage

**Don't take loans just to diversify.** But if you need a loan anyway, having mix helps.

### 12. Keep Old Accounts Active

Use old credit cards occasionally (small purchase, pay immediately).

**Why:** Inactive accounts may be closed by banks, removing positive history.

## Special Situations

### After AKPK (DMP Completion)

If you completed AKPK's Debt Management Programme:

1. Get completion letter from AKPK
2. All accounts should show as "settled"
3. Start rebuilding with secured card or small loan
4. Some banks accept AKPK graduates — try AEON, Bank Rakyat
5. Full recovery takes 2-3 years of good behavior

### After Bankruptcy Discharge

1. Get discharge certificate
2. Open basic savings account
3. After 6-12 months, try secured credit card
4. Build history slowly over 2-3 years
5. Banks will be cautious — licensed lenders more flexible initially

### After Settlement (Paid Less Than Owed)

1. "Settled" is better than "outstanding" but not as good as "paid in full"
2. Keep settlement letter forever
3. Continue building positive history
4. Impact diminishes over 2-3 years

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does bad credit stay on record?
CCRIS shows 12 months of payment history. However, serious issues (defaults, legal cases) can remain visible longer. There's no automatic 7-year wipe like in some countries.

### Can I pay to remove negative records?
No. Only legitimate corrections (errors, fraud) can be removed. Accurate negative information stays until it naturally ages out or account is settled.

### Does checking my own credit hurt my score?
No. Self-checks are "soft inquiries" with no impact. Check as often as you want.

### How much can my score improve in 6 months?
With perfect behavior: 50-100 points possible on CTOS. Depends heavily on your starting point and what's dragging you down.

### Should I pay off old debts that are already written off?
Usually yes. "Settled" looks better than "written off" to lenders. But negotiate — you can often settle for less than full amount.

### Will closing unused credit cards help?
Usually no. Closing cards reduces your total available credit, which increases utilization percentage. Keep old cards open with zero balance.
